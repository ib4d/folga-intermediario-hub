# Pricing Objection Handling

Use this for live calls, email follow-up, and negotiation.

## "It is expensive"

Use:

`It is priced as an operational system, not a cheap generic CRM. If it removes document chaos, reduces manual coordination, and gives your team one real operating flow, the cost is usually justified by saved time and fewer operational errors.`

## "We want to start cheaper"

Use:

`The cleanest way to start cheaper is to reduce scope, not to pretend the same scope can be supported at a lower commercial level. We can keep the first pilot narrow and validate the core workflow properly.`

## "Can you discount it?"

Use:

`Possibly, but I only use early-adopter pricing when there is real strategic value, case-study value, or fast-close commitment. I prefer to protect the rollout quality first.`

## "We just want to try it on our own"

Use:

`At this stage we run guided pilots because the value comes from validating the workflow with your real team, not from dropping a tool into your lap and hoping adoption happens by itself.`

## "We already use Excel / WhatsApp"

Use:

`That usually works until the team size, document volume, and legal/logistics complexity make visibility and accountability too weak. The issue is not whether Excel works at all, but whether it still works safely at your current scale.`

## "We need unlimited support"

Use:

`Unlimited support is not part of the standard offer. If you need higher-touch support, we scope it explicitly instead of hiding it inside a standard price.`

## "Can you include customization?"

Use:

`Standard pilot and rollout pricing covers the core operating model. Custom development or unusual customization is quoted separately so the commercial scope stays honest.`
