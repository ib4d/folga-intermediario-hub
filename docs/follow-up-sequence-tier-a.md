# Follow-up Sequence - Tier A

Use this sequence after the initial outreach batches.

Rule:

- do not spam
- do not send long essays
- do not reveal pricing before fit is acknowledged

## Touch 1 - Initial outreach

Already defined in:

- `docs/first-outreach-batch-tier-a.md`
- `docs/second-outreach-batch-tier-a.md`

## Touch 2 - Short follow-up

Send after:

- 3 to 5 working days

Template:

`Hi [Name], just following up in case the earlier note got buried. The reason I reached out is that teams handling international recruitment often hit an operational bottleneck around documents, legal readiness, and cross-team visibility long before they realize it. Happy to show the workflow briefly if relevant.`

## Touch 3 - Role/pain reframing

Send after:

- another 4 to 5 working days

Template:

`Hi [Name], a more direct version of my note: we are not trying to replace sourcing effort with another generic tool. ORI CRUIT HUB is meant for the operating layer where candidates, documents, legal follow-up, and logistics become hard to coordinate across teams. If that is not a live issue on your side, no problem.`

## Touch 4 - Soft break-up

Send after:

- another 5 to 7 working days

Template:

`Hi [Name], I will close the loop after this note. I reached out because your workflow looked like the kind of environment where document-heavy recruitment, legalisation, and operational readiness can become fragmented across too many channels. If it becomes relevant later, I would be happy to reconnect.`

## Optional branch - Replied but not committed

If the prospect replies but stays vague:

Use:

`Understood. The easiest next step is a short guided walkthrough focused only on whether the current workflow is actually painful enough to justify a pilot. If the fit is weak, we stop there.`

## Optional branch - Price appears too early

If they ask price before fit is established:

Use:

`We usually start with a guided pilot and then move into rollout pricing depending on scope. I prefer first confirming whether the workflow fit is real, because the right commercial structure depends on the operating complexity and adoption path.`

## Optional branch - Wants something cheaper

Use:

`If budget is the issue, the cleanest move is to reduce scope first rather than pretend the same rollout can be supported at a lower level. We can keep the validation narrow and serious.`

## Sequence rule

Stop after Touch 4 unless:

- they reply
- there is a warm referral
- the account is strategically important enough to revisit later with a different role
