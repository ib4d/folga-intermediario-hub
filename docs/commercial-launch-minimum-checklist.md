# Commercial Launch Minimum Checklist

This is the final minimum checklist before actively pushing ORI CRUIT HUB into
guided selling.

It does not describe a broad SaaS launch.

It defines the minimum viable commercial package for:

- finding leads
- running guided demos
- qualifying pilots
- converting pilots into paid rollout

## 3. Lead -> demo -> pilot -> rollout sequence

Use this exact progression.

### Stage 1: lead

Goal:

- confirm that the agency has the problem strongly enough

Ask:

- are documents still handled through WhatsApp, email, or Excel
- where does legal follow-up get blocked
- who owns logistics readiness
- how many people touch the same candidate flow

Exit condition:

- there is enough operational pain to justify a demo

### Stage 2: guided demo

Goal:

- show that the platform replaces fragmented operations with one flow

Show:

- candidate view
- document upload
- OCR-assisted review
- legal signal
- logistics signal

Exit condition:

- the prospect wants to validate the workflow on their own team

### Stage 3: pilot qualification

Goal:

- decide whether the account deserves onboarding effort

Require:

- one owner on the customer side
- willingness to work in a guided way
- real operational use case
- real commercial intent

Exit condition:

- pilot approved or intentionally declined

### Stage 4: guided pilot

Goal:

- validate that the team uses the platform in real work

Track:

- active operators
- documents uploaded
- OCR reviews completed
- legal/logistics follow-up in app
- whether the team asks to expand usage

Exit condition:

- convert to paid rollout or stop cleanly

### Stage 5: paid rollout

Goal:

- extend from pilot success into a real customer relationship

Require:

- explicit commercial terms
- workflow already adopted
- customer owner aligned
- clear next-scope definition

Exit condition:

- paid production use begins with a defined operating model
