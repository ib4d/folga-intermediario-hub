# Pricing Deck - Guided Motion

This document is the recommended pricing deck for the current stage of ORI
CRUIT HUB.

It is built for:

- guided demos
- guided pilots
- paid rollout after validation

It is not built for broad self-serve SaaS positioning yet.

## 1. Market anchor

Current market references support premium operational pricing:

- horizontal business software often starts around `$25-$100/user/month`
- Workable is positioned around `$299-$599/month` per company for hiring use
- Sugar-type mid-market CRM pricing reaches `~$59-$135/user/month`
- higher-complexity platforms can impose annual minimums around `$10,000+`

For ORI CRUIT HUB, this matters because the product is not a lightweight CRM.
It is a vertical operating system for cross-border recruitment operations with:

- candidates
- documents
- OCR-assisted review
- legal follow-up
- logistics follow-up

That supports pricing above cheap SMB SaaS tools, especially when the sale is
guided and onboarding effort is real.

## 2. Recommended commercial stance

Recommended stance:

`Price like a specialized operational system with guided rollout, not like a cheap generic SaaS tool.`

## 3. Recommended offer ladder

### Guided Pilot

- setup fee: `EUR 2,500`
- pilot cycle fee: `EUR 2,000 / 30 days`
- included: `up to 5 operators`

Use when:

- the customer wants validation before broader commitment

### Core Rollout

- `EUR 1,250 / month`
- included: `up to 5 operators`

Use when:

- one team is running the platform in steady production use

### Growth Rollout

- `EUR 2,500 / month`
- included: `up to 15 operators`

Use when:

- the agency has meaningful internal coordination needs across recruiters,
  document review, legal, and logistics

### Scale Rollout

- `EUR 4,500 / month`
- included: `up to 30 operators`

Use when:

- multiple teams or offices operate through the same flow

### Enterprise

- `from EUR 7,500 / month`

Use when:

- scope, support, or deployment expectations move beyond the standard guided model

## 4. Discount discipline

Recommended discount logic:

- do not discount first price reflexively
- use discounts only for:
  - strategic logos
  - strong referral value
  - fast-close commitment
  - unusually strong case-study potential

Recommended floor:

- guided pilot floor: `EUR 1,500 setup + EUR 1,250 / 30 days`
- core rollout floor: `EUR 990 / month`

Do not normalize lower than that without a deliberate reason.

## 5. Why this pricing is defensible

It is defensible because the product value is not just software access.

The customer is buying:

- operational control
- lower document chaos
- reduced Excel/WhatsApp dependency
- better legal/logistics coordination
- guided adoption instead of abandoned self-serve

In other words, price should reflect avoided operational loss, not only feature count.

## 6. Recommended talk track

Use:

`We price the pilot as a guided validation phase, because the goal is to prove the workflow with your team in production conditions.`

Use:

`After validation, pricing moves into a defined rollout scope based on operator count and operating complexity.`

Use:

`We are deliberately not pretending this is a cheap no-touch tool. It is an operational control system.`

## 7. What not to do

Do not:

- price like a commodity SMB CRM
- publish very low entry pricing now
- bundle unlimited support by default
- promise enterprise custom work inside standard tiers

## 8. Recommended immediate choice

If you want one practical starting position right now:

- pilot offer list price: `EUR 2,500 setup + EUR 2,000 / 30 days`
- first rollout anchor: `EUR 1,250 / month`
- growth anchor: `EUR 2,500 / month`

That is high enough to position the product seriously, while still being
defensible for agencies with real operational pain.
