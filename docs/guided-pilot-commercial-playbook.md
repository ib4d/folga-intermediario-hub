# Guided Pilot Commercial Playbook

This document defines the minimum commercial-operational model for selling ORI
CRUIT HUB now, without pretending the product is already a no-touch self-serve
SaaS.

It is the practical answer to:

- what exactly we are offering
- how a first pilot should start
- what success looks like
- when a pilot is ready to convert into a paid rollout

## 1. Current commercial mode

Current mode:

`Guided pilot with supported rollout.`

This means:

- demo can be self-initiated or guided
- onboarding is still guided
- production runtime is real
- operator review remains part of the OCR workflow where needed
- commercial activation is assisted, not fully hands-off

## 2. What we are selling right now

Sell this:

- a guided pilot for one agency team
- controlled onboarding into one production tenant
- support for the first operational flows
- document, legal, and logistics usage with real operator supervision

Do not sell this yet:

- blind self-serve onboarding for any customer
- fully productized self-serve subscription billing
- zero-review OCR automation
- enterprise-grade observability promises

## 3. Recommended pilot offer structure

Use a simple offer shape:

1. discovery and qualification
2. guided demo
3. pilot decision
4. controlled onboarding
5. short pilot cycle
6. convert to paid rollout or stop cleanly

## 4. Qualification gate before offering a pilot

Offer the pilot only if the prospect has at least most of these signals:

- manages real candidate volume
- already suffers from Excel/WhatsApp fragmentation
- has at least one recruiter or operator who will actively use the tool
- has document handling pain
- has legal/logistics coordination pain
- can commit to a guided rollout instead of waiting for a perfect self-serve product

If these signals are missing, stay at demo stage.

## 5. Minimum onboarding package for the first pilot

The first pilot should include:

- one active organization
- named operator owner on the customer side
- one primary workflow to validate
- one document flow to validate
- one legal or logistics checkpoint to validate
- one short review cadence during the pilot

## 6. Suggested pilot timeline

Keep the first pilot short and concrete.

Recommended rhythm:

- Week 0: guided demo and qualification
- Week 1: onboarding and first workflow activation
- Week 2-3: real usage with operator feedback
- Week 4: review decision

If the pilot needs longer because the customer is slow, extend deliberately.
Do not let it drift without a decision point.

## 7. What success looks like during the pilot

The pilot is working if most of these become true:

- the team uses the platform repeatedly, not just once
- candidates are being tracked in the system instead of only in chat
- documents are uploaded and reviewed in the app
- legal or logistics status is being used as part of real follow-up
- the customer sees operational clarity they did not have before
- the team asks to expand usage instead of asking to go back to Excel

## 8. Pilot metrics worth checking

Keep metrics simple at this stage.

Check:

- number of active operators
- number of candidates touched in the system
- number of documents uploaded
- number of OCR reviews completed
- number of legal/logistics status updates made in-app
- whether the customer wants another internal team member added

## 9. Conversion gate from pilot to paid rollout

Move from pilot to paid rollout when:

- the operational owner wants to keep using the system
- at least one real workflow is already running inside the app
- the app is saving coordination effort or reducing document chaos
- the customer accepts the current guided operating model
- the commercial terms are explicit

Do not convert if:

- usage is only theatrical demo usage
- the customer expects fully hands-off SaaS immediately
- the team never adopted the workflow in practice
- the value case remains vague

## 10. Recommended commercial language

Use:

`We onboard pilots in a guided way so your team can adopt the workflow without operational disruption.`

Use:

`The current product is already live in production, and the pilot lets you validate the flow with real operators and real work.`

Use:

`OCR accelerates document handling, while the final compliance review stays with your team when needed.`

Avoid:

- `fully automatic OCR`
- `plug-and-play self-serve for every agency`
- `finished enterprise billing rollout`
- `broad mass distribution already closed`

## 11. Stop conditions

Stop a pilot cleanly if:

- there is no real operator engagement
- the prospect cannot assign ownership
- the team refuses guided rollout discipline
- the commercial intent is not serious enough to justify rollout effort

Stopping early is better than carrying dead pilots.

## 12. Recommended current decision

Commercially, the clean position is:

`Sell guided pilots now. Convert successful pilots into paid rollout. Delay self-serve SaaS positioning until billing, onboarding, and OCR claims are genuinely closed.`
