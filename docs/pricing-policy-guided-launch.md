# Pricing Policy - Guided Launch

This document is the negotiation and pricing policy for the current commercial
stage of ORI CRUIT HUB.

It defines:

- list price
- early-adopter price
- hard floor
- what is included
- what is extra
- when discounts are allowed

## 1. Pricing posture

Posture:

`Premium but defensible.`

Reason:

- the product solves operational coordination, not just record keeping
- onboarding is guided
- OCR/document handling is part of the value
- legal/logistics coordination increases operational leverage

Do not sell it like a cheap generic SaaS.

## 2. Official list price

### Guided Pilot

- setup fee: `EUR 2,500`
- pilot cycle fee: `EUR 2,000 / 30 days`
- included operators: `up to 5`

### Paid Rollout - Core

- `EUR 1,250 / month`
- included operators: `up to 5`

### Paid Rollout - Growth

- `EUR 2,500 / month`
- included operators: `up to 15`

### Paid Rollout - Scale

- `EUR 4,500 / month`
- included operators: `up to 30`

### Enterprise

- `from EUR 7,500 / month`

## 3. Early-adopter offer

Use only for the first strategic customers or high-value references.

### Guided Pilot

- setup fee: `EUR 1,500`
- pilot cycle fee: `EUR 1,250 / 30 days`

### Paid Rollout - Core

- `EUR 990 / month`

Rule:

Do not automatically extend early-adopter pricing to every prospect.

## 4. Hard floor

Below this level, the deal usually stops making strategic sense.

### Guided Pilot hard floor

- setup fee: `EUR 1,500`
- pilot cycle fee: `EUR 1,250 / 30 days`

### Rollout hard floor

- core rollout: `EUR 990 / month`

Only go below hard floor if all of these are true:

- strategic logo value is unusually high
- there is clear referral leverage
- there is serious fast-close commitment
- there is explicit founder approval

## 5. What is included by default

### Pilot includes

- 1 tenant
- up to 5 operators
- 1 guided onboarding session
- 1 weekly review cadence
- 1 agreed pilot scope

### Core rollout includes

- 1 production tenant
- up to 5 operators
- standard workflow scope
- standard production support expectation

## 6. What is extra and billable

These are not included by default:

- additional operator packs: `EUR 300 / month per 5 operators`
- extra tenant / extra brand environment: `from EUR 750 / month`
- premium onboarding / migration project: `from EUR 2,000 one-time`
- white-label / branded rollout setup: `from EUR 1,500 one-time`
- custom reporting / export package: `from EUR 500 / month`
- higher-touch support cadence: `from EUR 750 / month`
- custom development: `quoted separately`

## 7. Discount policy

Allowed discount reasons:

- strategic logo
- strong case-study potential
- strong referral potential
- fast-close commitment

Bad discount reasons:

- prospect asks reflexively
- vague future promises
- no owner on the customer side
- no real workflow pain

## 8. Negotiation sequence

Use this sequence:

1. present list price confidently
2. restate value in operational terms
3. narrow scope if needed before lowering price
4. use early-adopter price only if strategically justified
5. never jump directly below the floor

## 9. Scope before discount rule

If the prospect resists price:

- first reduce scope
- then reduce support intensity
- only then consider discount

Do not give lower price while keeping premium scope and premium support.

## 10. Recommended default commercial path

Default path:

1. sell pilot at list price
2. if needed, use early-adopter pricing selectively
3. convert into Core or Growth rollout

## 11. Current recommended operating rule

Use this rule:

`Anchor high, defend the value, reduce scope before discount, and protect the floor.`
